The first step of exploration is asking the right questions about our universe.
When we look up at the night sky, we are not just seeing stars—we are reading a
history written in light, spanning lifetimes and distances that are hard to imagine.

Space probes expand our reach by traveling where humans cannot. They carry instruments
that measure radiation, magnetic fields, and the chemistry of distant worlds. Every
transmission back to Earth is a tiny message from another environment, turned into data
we can analyze and learn from.

To build a future for space exploration, we combine engineering, navigation, and careful
science. Missions plan routes, manage risk, and keep instruments calibrated so results
from Mars, the Moon, or the outer planets can be compared and understood over time.
